## Deliberately temporary

- Primitive character, enemy, weapon, and prop geometry remains in place until the final art pipeline lands.
- The four major-process silhouettes communicate mechanics and collision only; they are not antagonist concept art.
- The headquarters is a functional interface rather than a final authored 3D location.
- Approximate room collision and direct enemy navigation are still used.
- Sound and music are not wired.
- Run results, profile, archive, and purchases use browser-local storage adapters.
- Location route names currently alter systems parameters, not authored cultural environment kits.
- Gameplay balance, process timing, appraisal values, route rewards, and upgrade costs are first-pass.

## Immediate engineering risks

- Wall visuals and navigable openings are not fully derived from corridor cuts.
- Enemy navigation can crowd narrow corridors and needs graph-aware steering.
- No automated browser-play test or deterministic combat replay test exists yet.
- No pause, settings, controller, mobile-control, or accessibility interface exists yet.
- Local storage has migration but no cloud synchronization, conflict resolution, or recovery export.
- Headquarters transactions need UI playtesting for readability and economic pacing.
- Route parameters require hands-on evaluation to ensure they produce meaningfully different runs.
- The Seizure Chief must be tested for confiscation clarity and fair recovery windows.
- The Route Runner needs collision and teleport-location testing on narrow generated overlaps.
- The Warden requires extraction-lock pacing tests so players understand why the portal refuses them.
- All four major processes need full encounter tuning across early and late profile states.
